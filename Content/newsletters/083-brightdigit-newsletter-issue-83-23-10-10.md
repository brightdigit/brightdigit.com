Hello everyone,

  

Progress on Bushel is moving along and I’m enjoying my new iPhone 15 Pro
Max and Apple Watch Ultra 2. As autumn comes to the Midwest and we get
to enjoy more delicious apple products 🥧 🍩 🍺, I wanted to talk about
keeping your developers happy.

  

<a href="http://brightdigit.com/articles/humane-code/"
target="_blank">In the latest article we talk about <strong>Humane
Code.</strong></a> What is it?

**Humane code** takes code that is easy to understand, cheap to
maintain, better documented and has a longer lifespan further.

<a href="https://youtu.be/YVrHPCZnC50" style="display:block"
target="_blank" data-block-id="53"><img
src="https://mcusercontent.com/cb3bba007ed171091f55c47f0/video_thumbnails_new/23c991de24369a3d89d442b72cb8d1b9.png"
style="border:0;width:564px;height:auto;max-width:100%;display:block"
width="564" alt="Humane Development with Jill Scott" /></a>

Humane code appears to have been first coined by Danish-based software
architect Mark Seemann in his book *Code That Fits In Your Head*. The
book is essentially a guide to reducing code complexity and building
software at a sustainable, human pace.

At its core, **humane code is based on the idea that code is a
conversation.** This conversation is between you as the developer
writing it, a future version of yourself who will need to review and
explain it, and other developers who will need to use and maintain what
you create.

Read more in <a href="http://brightdigit.com/articles/humane-code/"
target="_blank"><strong>our latest article here.</strong></a>

Leo
