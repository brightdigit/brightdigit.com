Hello everyone,

  

I’ve been continuing my work on <a href="https://getbushel.app"
target="_blank"><strong>Bushel</strong></a> lately and using the latest
technologies from this year’s WWDC. As you may know I like breaking my
codebase into smaller pieces. This is where a concept I’ve learned from
<a
href="https://brightdigit.com/episodes/123-microapps-architecture-with-majid-jabrayilov/"
target="_blank">Majid Jabrayilov</a> and <a
href="https://brightdigit.com/episodes/093-test-driven-development-in-swift-with-gio-lodi/"
target="_blank">Gio Lodi</a> comes in
-<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
target="_blank"><strong>Microapps</strong></a>.

<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
style="display:block" target="_blank" data-block-id="49"><img
src="https://dim.mcusercontent.com/cs/cb3bba007ed171091f55c47f0/images/d01b0207-ccb7-e4c0-7675-490274744080.png?w=960&amp;dpr=2"
style="border:0;width:660px;height:auto;max-width:100%;max-height:100%;border-radius:0;display:block"
width="660" alt="Bushel Code Base" /></a>

Rather than building a monolithic app, you can break down the
functionality into lightweight modules.
<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
target="_blank"><strong>Microapps</strong></a> are a way of organizing
that.

<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
target="_blank"><strong>In the latest article</strong></a> we talk about
the benefits, both business and technical, of microapp architecture and
how to decide whether it might be a good choice for your development
needs.

<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
style="display:block" target="_blank" data-block-id="51"><img
src="https://dim.mcusercontent.com/cs/cb3bba007ed171091f55c47f0/images/0aa7b515-f953-af17-a610-58b7010ed81b.jpeg?w=564&amp;dpr=2"
style="border:0;width:660px;height:auto;max-width:100%;max-height:100%;border-radius:16px;display:block"
width="660" alt="How to automate iOS development" /></a>

*The biggest and most obvious benefit of*
<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
target="_blank"><em>microapps</em></a> *is that when your entire app is
broken down into single-function parts, building each part, testing it,
and adjusting it as needed can be done very rapidly.*

*…*

*This also makes microapp architecture a good choice if you’re
developing an app that:*

-   *Requires a lot of small, discrete tasks embedded in the UI.*

-   *You plan to scale or add lots of additional functionality over its
    lifecycle.*

If you are looking for a way to better organize your apps, you’ll want
to check
<a href="https://brightdigit.com/articles/microapps-architecture/"
target="_blank"><strong>this article out.</strong></a>

Leo
